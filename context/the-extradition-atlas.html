Notes & caveats. "Treaty" means a bilateral extradition agreement in force, or coverage under a multilateral framework (e.g. the European Arrest Warrant for EU members; the UK–EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement post-Brexit). "Reluctant" flags countries that have a treaty on paper but have refused or consistently obstructed extradition in practice. "No treaty" does not mean extradition is impossible—some states cooperate on an ad-hoc reciprocity basis—but it removes the legal obligation. Data is synthesised from the US State Department list (18 U.S.C. § 3181), Wikipedia's extradition treaty compilations, and Eurojust / NCA guidance on the EAW and TCA. Treaty status for some smaller states (particularly former British colonies covered by historic 1931/1935 agreements) is inferred from legacy instruments and may have been superseded by domestic law. This tool is for illustration, not legal advice.
