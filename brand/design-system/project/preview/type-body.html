Why the median commute shrank six and a half minutes

A decade-long drift, hidden inside the averages, has finally surfaced — and it rewrites how we plan cities.

Between 2014 and 2024, the median one-way commute across fourteen OECD economies fell by six and a half minutes. The mean barely moved. The gap — between what the typical worker experiences and what the average suggests — is the story this week.

Averages are a polite conspiracy between the top and the bottom of a distribution. When a handful of exurban super-commuters stretch their journeys past two hours, the mean clings high even while the bulk of workers drift closer to home. That drift is what policy has missed.
