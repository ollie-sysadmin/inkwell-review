The brief, in one sentence

Insight for people who pay attention — charts worth repeating at dinner.

Inkwell Review is a twice-weekly publication that turns statistical peculiarities of human behaviour into charts people remember. This is its visual system — typography, papers, wordmark, chart card, article page — treated as one thing.

The system lives in a photocopied zine register. Ink is #111111, paper is #E1E1E1, nothing is pure black or pure white. Hierarchy is carried by one pastel paper per section, wall-to-wall — never mixed on the same page. Ink stays constant on every surface.

Type is deliberate. Atkinson Hyperlegible Soft for everything readable — ExtraBold for display, Regular for body. DM Serif Display for the wordmark, the giant footer, italic straplines and the editor's voice. JetBrains Mono only for tabular numerals. No icon mark. Ollie is a column, signed off with an em-dash.

Caveats & substitutions

  • No icon mark. The wordmark is the entire brand. If a circular app icon is needed, stamp the italic R from Review on a paper colour — do not invent a symbol.
  • No icon set shipped. Substitute Lucide (stroke 1.5, 18–20 px, currentColor) until real editorial icons are commissioned. Emoji never.
  • No photography shipped. Imagery direction is in the README; no assets are included.
  • Chart is a demonstrator. Production chart templates live in Astro later; this kit establishes the style, not the full library.
  • Ollie is a placeholder hole. The brand card shows the size and position; the portrait itself is a separate commission.
