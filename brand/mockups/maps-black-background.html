Maps on black — testing palette consistency across desks

Today's live template gives every map a desk-coloured background — Data is blue, Cover is purple, and so on. The two pieces below test the alternative: ink-black plates across every map, with the six brand paper colours doing the work at country level. Same status palette in the same positions, regardless of desk.

The three variants — trade-offs

A · Solid on black. Map reads as a printer's lithograph — black plate, ink colours pop. Strong consistency across desks. The chrome (kicker swatch, masthead, headline marks) retains the desk colour, the chart never does. Photocopy hand is preserved in the chrome but the chart itself is clean.

B · Hatched on black. Same as A but with photocopy-white hatching layered into the colour fills. Keeps the Chalabi/Bickford-Smith hand on the chart, at the cost of a busier read. White-line hatching is a slightly different print idiom (negative-space lithograph) than the original ink-on-paper hatching.

C · White plate, ink no-record. Highest legibility for the coloured tiers — paper colours read truer on white. Anchor uses an inset white halo to stay distinct from the no-record ink countries. But: no-record countries become visually loud (Africa, Russia, Saudi peninsula become large ink masses). For Atlas this is editorially apt — "no treaty = unreachable" is a meaningful negative the reader should feel. For Fugitive Destinations it's noisier — "no record" really means "no data," and the reader's eye is drawn to the absence rather than the colour. So C carries an editorial connotation that A doesn't.

Other open questions, all variants. The stat-strip misregistration still uses --desk, so the chrome retains its desk hue regardless of which variant wins. Tooltip background stays photocopy-white per Dene's call.
