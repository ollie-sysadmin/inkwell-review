The 78 countries that will not send you home.

Every extradition treaty on Earth, mapped. Select an anchor — and the network re-centres on it.

By Ollie Inkwell· 19 April 2026· 12 min read· Interactive map
Fig. I The Extradition Atlas — anchored on the United States
Russia — no treaty, the archetypal haven.
UAE — selective enforcement despite a 2008 bilateral.
Cuba — reluctant since 1926.

Extradition is the quiet infrastructure of cross-border enforcement. A treaty obliges one state to surrender a wanted person to another; without one, the receiving state is under no duty to help.

The United States has bilateral treaties with 117 countries. That leaves 78 where no such obligation exists — the map is thinnest where the headlines are loudest.

Classifying key

Anchor 1 The inquiring state.
Treaty in force 117 Bilateral or multilateral obligation recognised.
Reluctant 10 Treaty exists; rarely honoured in practice.
No treaty 78 No obligation. Cooperation is ad-hoc or absent.
