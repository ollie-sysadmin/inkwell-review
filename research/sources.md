*Data sources, journalists, and organisations that inform Inkwell Review's work. Used by `/scan` and `/produce` when assembling evidence.*

## Data repositories (start here)

**Global statistics:**
- [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org) — the gold standard
- [World Bank Open Data](https://data.worldbank.org)
- [UN DESA](https://www.un.org/development/desa/en/data.html) — population, migration
- [OECD Data](https://data.oecd.org) — rich countries, highly structured
- [Statista](https://statista.com) — paywalled but comprehensive
- [Gapminder](https://gapminder.org/data) — Rosling-era dataset library

**Crime & justice:**
- UNODC (UN Office on Drugs and Crime)
- Eurojust (European judicial cooperation)
- Transparency International CPI
- Prison Policy Initiative (US)
- World Prison Brief

**Health & mortality:**
- WHO Global Health Observatory
- CDC WONDER (US)
- IHME Global Burden of Disease

**Economic & financial:**
- IMF Data
- Forbes Billionaires
- Credit Suisse Global Wealth Report
- Knight Frank Wealth Report
- Piketty / Saez / Zucman World Inequality Database

**Cultural & behavioural:**
- Pew Research — global surveys
- YouGov — public opinion
- Gallup World Poll
- OECD Time Use Database
- Eurobarometer

**Names, demographics, language:**
- SSA Baby Names (US)
- ONS Baby Names (UK)
- Ethnologue (languages)

## Institutions to cite (build credibility)

When a piece references one of these, credibility rises materially:

- Academic journals (Nature, Lancet, AER)
- National statistical offices (BLS, ONS, Eurostat)
- Central banks (Fed, ECB, BIS)
- Supreme/Constitutional courts (for legal data)
- Major NGOs with research arms (IISS, Brookings, CFR, Chatham House)

---

## Sources to avoid

- Reddit threads as primary sources
- Wikipedia *alone* (fine as a starting point, never as the only citation)
- Aggregator sites with no upstream source
- "Studies show…" articles without links to the study
- Influencer charts without visible methodology
- AI-generated summaries without the original source

---

## When sources conflict

If two authoritative sources disagree:
1. Cite both
2. Note the discrepancy in the caption or footnote
3. Default to the more recent source
4. If the discrepancy is the story, lean into it
